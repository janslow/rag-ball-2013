“So come with me,
where dreams are born
and time is never planned”
J.M. Barrie

On November 23rd, let time stand still as you leave Oxford behind, and come away to Neverland with RAG Ball 2013. Enter a world where imagination takes over and adventure is around every corner. Feel the everyday melt away as you escape into a land somewhere between dream and reality.

Upon entering University Clubs, you will find yourself on the streets of Kensington with a glass of champagne. In no time you will be exploring the delights of Neverwood and relaxing in the music of the acoustic Enchanted Cave. Quench your thirst in the Mermaid Lagoon, and be pampered like never before in the Pixie Hollow. Whether you choose to dance the night away under the Neverland Sky, walk on water in Marooners' Rock, or risk everything on red aboard the Jolly Roger, Neverland has something for everyone.

Lose yourself in this fantasy, where youth is eternal, and mystery is everywhere. Neverland is waiting for you.

Entertainment

Upon arrival in Kensington you are greeted with a Treasure Map depicting an unknown land full of marvel, magic and mystery...

“Second to the right, and straight on till morning. That, Peter had told Wendy, was the way to the Neverland.”

The first glimpse of Neverland is the luscious Neverwood. Venturing through the foliage, you encounter forest folk - a henna artist and face painter. You continue on your way in search of the lost treasure...

Wander down into The Enchanted Cave and you will be immediately captivated by an eclectic array of acoustic acts including the phenomenally talented Jake Morley.

Leaving the music behind, you spy spiralling smoke in the distance. Relax with a Shisha pipe or take a trip to the Lost Boy's Hideout and go wild on the Bungee Run, Gladiator Joust and much more.

Your quest for treasure leads you upstairs, to Pixie Hollow where you take a well-deserved break and, after pampering, massages and hairdressing, you to return to your quest.

Whilst sounds of cosmic Funk, soul and latin styles float over the hills, illusions of a magician mask the way ahead and leave you stumbling onto the bow of a ship...

Finally, on the top deck aboard the Jolly Roger you find what you have been looking for. In between the poker tables and roulette wheels, lying untouched is the treasure!

Feeling elated, you find the silent disco and end your journey with a survivors photo with the friends that have stayed with you every step of the way!

With still more surprises that are yet to be revealed in Neverland... what will you discover there?

Charities

RAG Ball is Oxford University's largest student charity event, and the only ball giving every penny of profit to good causes. Last year, Oxford students raised over £11,000 through RAG Ball, a total this year's committee is working tirelessly to beat.

At the core of RAG Ball is our commitment to fundraising for Oxford RAG's four student chosen charities, and promoting engagement between Oxford RAG and students. RAG Ball 2013 is also working with OUSU's Environment and Ethics campaign to become the first Sustain-a-Ball, and limit our environmental impact and supporting local business.

By buying your ticket, not only are you guaranteed an incredible evening, you're also donating to four very different, very important charities:

Education Partnership Africa

 

Education Partnerships Africa is a UK Charity and East African NGO working hard to improve the standard of education open to young people in Kenya and Uganda. Students from Oxford, Cambridge, London and Manchester Universities take the opportunity to go and work within rural secondary schools every year, helping to equip and develop disadvantaged schools.

Over the past five years, EPAfrica has impacted the lives of over 10,000 Kenyan students, spent £9,000 on textbooks, £12,000 on school buildings and £5,000 on science equipment with the aim of increasing grades and raising aspirations in rural secondary schools. The money raised by RAG Ball will help to continue this work, and make a real difference to Kenyan and Ugandan students.

 

Students Supporting Street Kids

 

SSSK estimates that there are approximately 100 million street children around the world, vulnerable to all kinds of physical danger and exploitation by employers. Many end up on the streets because of family breakdown, acute poverty or local conflict, surviving often by begging or rag-picking, and worst cases turning to prostitution and crime.

SSSK is a small, student-run charity, emphasising the potential of street children, rather than presenting them as victims. SSSK supports NGO's in Egypt, Ethiopia, Cambodia, Kosovo, and Columbia amongst others. Each NGO has a strong relationship with the SSSK, ensuring that every penny of money raised is spent with the aim of improving the lives of street kids. The work of SSSK's chosen NGO's encourages advocacy for street kids, and projects that provide stability and shelter.

 

The Oxford Food Bank

 

The Oxford Food Bank works with local food suppliers to collect good quality fresh food, which is then donated to local registered charities for free! For every £1 donated to the Oxford Food Bank, £25 worth of food is delivered – an amazing result for those Oxford community projects that depend upon the food bank.

The Oxford Food Bank works with the homeless, the elderly, those with mental health problems, children, families on low incomes and other vulnerable members of our Oxford's community.

 

Giving What We Can

 

Extreme poverty is one of the most important issues of our age. Of the 6.6 billion people in the world, every day 2.5 billion live on less than $2.50 a day, whilst more than 1 billion lack access to clean drinking water. Whilst the causes and scale of poverty is immense, Giving What We Can emphasises that the problem is solvable, and by giving a small amount to effective charities, each of us can still transform the lives of hundreds of people.

Giving What We Can works in two ways; firstly it innovatively identifies the most efficient and effective charities tackling extreme poverty. Secondly, it encourages its members to donate 10% of their income to charity, which so far has led to a pledge total of $138 million – which although impressive is still a fraction of what's needed to end extreme poverty.
